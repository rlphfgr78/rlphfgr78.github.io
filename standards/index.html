Standards and Frameworks

The Lunar Research Initiative develops independent, open frameworks that help unify lunar manufacturing practices. These standards connect mission requirements to practical design, qualification, and verification methods for lunar surface operations.

Unified Lunar Quality Framework (ULQF)

ULQF is a structured quality framework for lunar surface manufacturing. It links critical to quality measures, predictive signatures, environmental modifiers, and quality gates across mission phases. ULQF helps engineers control variation, interpret drift, and design verification plans that reflect real lunar risks.

ULQF includes a handbook, implementation guide, and supporting tools such as CTQ libraries and predictive signature tables.

Lunar Manufacturing Quality Architecture

The Lunar Manufacturing Quality Architecture defines how quality and risk align across mission tiers, from individual processes to integrated surface systems. It provides a consistent logic that connects requirements, risk controls, and verification activities into a single architecture.

This framework supports mission architects, systems engineers, and manufacturing leads who need a clear quality structure for lunar infrastructure projects.

Regolith Manufacturing Process Standards

These emerging standards describe expectations for regolith feedstock characterization, process capability, and equipment behavior under lunar conditions. The goal is to translate regolith variability into predictable, capable manufacturing routes.

The standards guide material preparation, mechanical testing, process qualification, and monitoring strategies for regolith based production.

Verification and Validation Frameworks

LRI develops verification templates and logic flows for lunar production systems. These frameworks map risks and CTQs to test plans, environmental exposures, and acceptance criteria.

They support consistent, evidence based verification across additive manufacturing, construction systems, utilities, and support equipment on the lunar surface.

Technical Memoranda and Templates

LRI publishes NASA style technical memoranda that formalize specific methods, such as drift detection algorithms, load modifier models, or verification checklists. Future releases will also include example CTQ lists, quality gate diagrams, and verification matrices that organizations can adapt for their own lunar projects.
